Data Champions Program

Train department leaders to drive DLP adoption

Program Overview

The Data Champions Program empowers department leaders to become advocates and experts in Data Loss Prevention within their organizations. Champions serve as the bridge between IT security teams and business units, driving DLP adoption, educating colleagues, and ensuring PDPA compliance across all departments.

What is a Data Champion?

A Data Champion is a designated representative from each department who receives specialized training in DLP principles, PDPA compliance, and data protection best practices. They act as the first point of contact for data protection questions, help implement policies, and promote a culture of data security.

Why Have Data Champions?

Faster DLP Adoption

Champions understand both technical requirements and department-specific needs, accelerating policy implementation by 40-60% compared to top-down approaches.

Reduced IT Burden

Decentralize data protection knowledge. Champions handle department-level questions and issues, freeing IT security teams to focus on strategic initiatives.

Better Compliance

Continuous monitoring and training by champions lead to 70% fewer PDPA violations and faster incident response times across the organization.

Cultural Transformation

Champions create a data-aware culture. Employees see data protection as everyone's responsibility, not just IT's problem.

Program Structure

Our comprehensive 12-week program transforms department representatives into certified Data Champions.

1

Weeks 1-2: Foundation

  • Introduction to DLP concepts and terminology
  • PDPA overview and requirements
  • Data classification fundamentals
2

Weeks 3-6: Technical Skills

  • DLP policy creation and management
  • Incident investigation and response
  • Data discovery and inventory tools
  • Department-specific data patterns
3

Weeks 7-9: Leadership & Communication

  • Training end users effectively
  • Change management strategies
  • Stakeholder communication
  • Creating awareness campaigns
4

Weeks 10-12: Practice & Certification

  • Hands-on labs and simulations
  • Department-specific use case development
  • Final assessment and certification exam
  • Ongoing support plan creation
