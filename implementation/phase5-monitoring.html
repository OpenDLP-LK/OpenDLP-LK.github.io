Phase 5: Continuous Monitoring

Operations & Improvement | Ongoing

Phase Overview

Phase 5 establishes ongoing DLP operations. This continuous phase focuses on 24/7 monitoring, incident response, compliance reporting, regular audits, and continuous improvement. Your DLP program evolves with changing threats, business needs, and PDPA requirements.

Duration

Ongoing / Permanent

Key Stakeholders

SOC Team, DPO, IT Security, Compliance

Success Criteria

Zero breaches, PDPA compliance, continuous improvement

Key Activities

24/7 Monitoring & Alerting

Continuous surveillance of DLP events, automated alerting, and real-time incident detection.

  • Monitor dashboards for policy violations and anomalies
  • Configure SIEM integration for centralized logging
  • Establish on-call rotation for critical alerts
  • Define escalation paths for different severity levels

Incident Response

Swift, coordinated response to data loss incidents per PDPA Section 11 requirements.

  • Investigate alerts within 1 hour of detection
  • Document all incidents in ticketing system
  • Notify PDPA Authority within 72 hours if breach confirmed
  • Conduct post-incident reviews and corrective actions

Reporting & Analytics

Regular reporting to stakeholders on DLP performance, trends, and compliance status.

  • Weekly security reports to CISO
  • Monthly compliance reports to Data Protection Officer
  • Quarterly executive dashboards with KPIs
  • Annual PDPA compliance attestation

Continuous Improvement

Regular policy reviews, technology updates, and process optimization based on learnings.

  • Quarterly policy reviews and updates
  • Annual risk assessments and gap analyses
  • Technology refresh cycles (every 2-3 years)
  • Training refresher programs

PDPA Ongoing Requirements

Phase 5 ensures continuous PDPA compliance through these ongoing activities:

Records of Processing Activities (Section 13)

Maintain detailed logs of all data processing activities, DLP policy enforcement, and access controls.

Data Breach Notification (Section 11)

Report breaches to PDPA Authority within 72 hours. Notify affected individuals without undue delay.

Regular Audits (Section 8)

Conduct annual internal audits of data protection practices. Engage external auditors every 2-3 years.

Training & Awareness (Section 4)

Provide annual data protection training to all employees. Specialized training for data handlers.

Incident Response Workflow

1

Detection & Triage (0-15 min)

DLP alert generated → Security analyst reviews → Severity assessment → Initial classification

2

Investigation (15-60 min)

Review logs → Interview involved users → Determine scope → Confirm if actual breach or false positive

3

Containment (1-4 hours)

Block further data loss → Disable accounts → Revoke access → Quarantine affected systems

4

Notification (within 72 hours)

Notify PDPA Authority if required → Inform affected data subjects → Report to management → Legal consultation

5

Recovery & Lessons Learned

Restore normal operations → Update policies → Conduct post-mortem → Implement preventive measures

Continuous Improvement Cycle

Measure

Collect metrics, analyze trends, benchmark against industry standards

Analyze

Identify gaps, review incidents, assess emerging threats

Improve

Update policies, enhance detection, optimize workflows

Repeat

Continuous cycle of measurement and improvement

Best Practices

Automate Where Possible

Use SOAR platforms to automate incident response, reporting, and routine tasks. Focus human effort on complex investigations.

Stay Current on Threats

Subscribe to security bulletins, attend conferences, participate in industry forums. Update detection patterns for new attack vectors.

Document Everything

Maintain comprehensive documentation for PDPA compliance audits. Record all policy changes, incidents, and reviews.

Foster Security Culture

Celebrate employees who report suspicious activity. Make security awareness part of company culture, not just a compliance checkbox.
