Phase 1: Planning

Strategic Foundation | Months 0-2

Phase Overview

The Planning phase establishes the strategic foundation for your DLP implementation. This critical first step involves defining objectives, securing stakeholder buy-in, assembling your team, and creating a comprehensive roadmap aligned with PDPA requirements.

Duration

2 months

Key Stakeholders

C-Suite, IT, Legal, HR, Compliance

Success Criteria

Approved charter, funded budget, team assembled

Key Objectives

1

Define Business Objectives

Establish clear goals for DLP implementation: PDPA compliance, intellectual property protection, customer data security, regulatory requirements.

2

Secure Executive Sponsorship

Obtain C-level commitment, budget approval, and organizational priority status for the DLP program.

3

Assemble Project Team

Form cross-functional team with representatives from IT Security, Compliance, Legal, HR, and key business units.

4

Conduct Initial Assessment

Evaluate current data protection posture, identify gaps, assess risks, and understand regulatory obligations under PDPA.

5

Create Implementation Roadmap

Develop detailed project plan with timelines, milestones, resource requirements, and success metrics for all five phases.

Phase Tasks

Weeks 1-2: Project Initiation

  • Develop business case and ROI justification for DLP investment
  • Present proposal to executive leadership and secure sponsorship
  • Obtain budget approval and resource allocation
  • Draft project charter with scope, objectives, and governance structure

Weeks 3-4: Team Formation

  • Identify and assign project manager and core team members
  • Define roles and responsibilities (RACI matrix)
  • Establish governance model and decision-making framework
  • Set up communication channels and collaboration tools

Weeks 5-6: Assessment & Analysis

  • Review PDPA requirements and assess compliance gaps
  • Conduct high-level data inventory and classification assessment
  • Identify critical data repositories and high-risk areas
  • Document current security controls and policies

Weeks 7-8: Roadmap Development

  • Create detailed implementation roadmap for all 5 phases
  • Define success metrics and KPIs for each phase
  • Develop risk mitigation strategies
  • Obtain stakeholder approval for project plan

PDPA Compliance Focus

Phase 1 establishes the foundation for PDPA compliance by ensuring your DLP program addresses key requirements:

Section 4: Data Protection Officer

Designate DPO and establish accountability framework

Section 7: Security Safeguards

Plan technical and organizational measures for data protection

Section 11: Data Breach Notification

Prepare incident response and notification framework

Section 13: Records Management

Plan documentation and audit trail requirements

Best Practices

Start with Executive Education

Brief leadership on PDPA requirements, potential fines (up to 5% of turnover), and business impact of data breaches before requesting budget.

Include Business Representatives

DLP impacts business operations. Include department heads in planning to ensure buy-in and practical implementation.

Document Everything

PDPA requires demonstrable accountability. Document all decisions, assessments, and planning activities from day one.
