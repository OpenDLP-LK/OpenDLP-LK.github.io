Phase 3: Policy Development

Rules & Controls | Months 4-6

Phase Overview

Phase 3 translates your data discovery findings into actionable DLP policies and rules. You'll create comprehensive policies covering data handling, acceptable use, incident response, and enforcement actions—all aligned with PDPA requirements and business needs.

Duration

2 months

Key Stakeholders

Legal, Compliance, IT Security, HR

Success Criteria

Approved policies, configured rules, user training plan

DLP Policy Categories

Data Protection Policies

Core policies defining how sensitive data must be handled, stored, and transmitted.

  • • Encryption requirements for data at rest and in transit
  • • Access control and authentication requirements
  • • Data retention and disposal procedures
  • • Backup and recovery requirements

Acceptable Use Policies

Rules governing employee use of company systems, data, and resources.

  • • Approved communication channels for sensitive data
  • • Personal device usage (BYOD) guidelines
  • • Cloud service and file sharing restrictions
  • • Social media and external communication policies

Data Transfer Policies

Controls for moving data internally and externally.

  • • Email attachment restrictions and encryption
  • • USB and removable media controls
  • • Cloud upload policies (Dropbox, Google Drive, etc.)
  • • Print and screen capture restrictions

Incident Response Policies

Procedures for detecting, responding to, and reporting data loss incidents.

  • • Alert escalation procedures
  • • Investigation and forensics processes
  • • PDPA breach notification requirements (72 hours)
  • • Remediation and corrective actions

Sample DLP Rules

Block NIC Numbers via Email

BLOCK

Prevent emails containing Sri Lankan NIC numbers from being sent to external recipients.

Condition: Email contains pattern matching NIC (old or new format) AND recipient domain is external
Action: Block and notify sender + security team

Encrypt Financial Reports

ENCRYPT

Automatically encrypt emails containing financial reports before external transmission.

Condition: Email contains keywords "financial report" OR "P&L statement" AND has external recipient
Action: Encrypt email + require password

Alert on Large Data Upload

ALERT

Monitor and alert when users upload large volumes of data to cloud services.

Condition: Upload to cloud service (Dropbox, OneDrive) > 100MB with Confidential classification
Action: Allow but alert security team for review

Policy Development Process

1

Draft Initial Policies

Use Phase 2 discovery findings and PDPA requirements to create first drafts. Start with templates and customize.

2

Stakeholder Review

Circulate to Legal, HR, Business Units, and IT for feedback. Ensure policies are enforceable and practical.

3

Technical Implementation Design

Translate policies into specific DLP rules, patterns, and actions. Configure detection logic and enforcement.

4

Executive Approval

Obtain formal sign-off from executive sponsors and legal counsel before deployment.

5

User Communication Plan

Develop training materials, FAQs, and communication strategy to prepare employees for policy rollout.

PDPA Compliance Integration

Ensure your DLP policies address these critical PDPA requirements:

Purpose Limitation (Section 5)

Policies must prevent data use beyond stated purposes. Restrict access based on role and need.

Data Minimization (Section 6)

Rules should encourage collecting only necessary data. Flag excessive data collection.

Security Safeguards (Section 7)

Enforce encryption, access controls, and secure transmission for personal data.

Transfer Restrictions (Section 9)

Control cross-border data transfers. Require approvals and safeguards.

Step-by-step procedures for handling DLP alerts and data breach incidents.

Best Practices

Start with Monitor Mode

Begin with alert-only policies before blocking actions. Tune rules based on false positives to avoid disrupting business.

Keep Policies Clear and Simple

Use plain language employees can understand. Complex, technical policies lead to confusion and violations.

Include Business Justification

Explain *why* each policy exists (PDPA compliance, IP protection). Understanding drives adoption.

Plan for Exceptions

Establish exception request process for legitimate business needs. Don't make policies so rigid they force workarounds.
